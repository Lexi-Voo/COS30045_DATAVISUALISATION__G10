About This Project

The Drug Testing Dashboard visualises trends in roadside drug testing across Australia between 2008 and 2024. It aims to make complex enforcement data accessible and interpretable to policymakers, researchers, and the public. This dashboard enables exploration of key metrics — total tests, detection rates, and jurisdictional comparisons — offering insights into the impact of drug-impaired driving and its enforcement.

Project Objectives

Track Key Trends

Monitor changes in total drug tests and positive detections over time to identify long-term enforcement impacts.

Identify Drug Patterns

Understand which drug types are most commonly detected and their distribution across demographics.

Compare Jurisdictions

Examine regional differences in testing intensity and outcomes across Australian states and territories.

Data Source

The data used in this dashboard is sourced from the Bureau of Infrastructure and Transport Research Economics (BITRE) Police Enforcement for Roadside Drug Tests collection. It includes the number of roadside tests, positive detections, and enforcement metrics between 2008 and 2024.

Access the dataset here: BITRE Road Safety Data Hub
